Ever feel like someone is watching you… but they’re also bad at hiding?

Meet **Mothman**, the world’s most anxious flying omen.

He appears in the dark, glowing red eyes, dramatic silhouette… very intimidating stuff.

But in reality:

He is just standing there… awkwardly… waiting for someone to notice him.

**Mothman’s Daily Struggles:**
Tries to be mysterious → accidentally gets spotted immediately
Shows up in ominous locations → nobody reacts fast enough
Wants to be terrifying prophecy → ends up being “that weird flying guy again”

You hear flapping outside your window at 2 a.m.

You look out.

He’s just hovering there like:

“Oh. Hi. I was trying to be scary. Did it work this time?”

**Folklore Fact:** Mothman is a cryptid from American folklore, often associated with mysterious sightings and urban legends. Descriptions vary, but it is commonly depicted as a winged humanoid with glowing red eyes.

Ever hear strange flapping sounds outside your window late at night…

…but it's moving suspiciously slow?

Meet the **Manananggal**, the world's most committed nighttime flyer.

By day, they look just like an ordinary person.

But at night...

They literally leave the bottom half of their body behind.

**Manananggal's Daily Struggles:**

* Tries to make a dramatic takeoff → remembers they forgot where they left their legs
* Flies silently through the night → wings make way more noise than expected
* Wants to be the stuff of nightmares → spends half the evening trying to land gracefully

You hear wings beating outside at 2 a.m.

You peek through the window.

The Manananggal is circling your neighborhood like:

"Hold on... I think I missed the right house..."

**Folklore Fact:** The Manananggal is a legendary creature from Philippine folklore. It is traditionally depicted as a person—usually a woman—who separates at the waist after dark, with the upper body growing large bat-like wings to fly through the night. In many stories, it is said to prey on sleeping people, especially pregnant women, using a long, hollow tongue. According to folklore, the creature can be defeated by sprinkling salt, garlic, or ash on the lower half of its body before it reunites at dawn.

**Question of the Day:**
If you saw a Manananggal flying overhead, would you:
A. Pretend you didn't see anything and go back inside
B. Watch from a safe distance because you're curious
C. Quietly grab some salt... just in case

*No lower halves were misplaced during the making of this post. The Manananggal eventually found where it parked its legs.* 🦇
